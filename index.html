About Us

STEAMHers is an organization dedicated to empowering women in Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics (STEAM). Founded by Shreya Baronia and Maider Escobar, STEAMHers aims to create a supportive community for women to thrive in traditionally male-dominated fields.

Our Mission

Our mission is to provide educational resources, mentorship, and networking opportunities to women and girls of all ages. We believe that by fostering a collaborative environment, we can inspire the next generation of female leaders in STEAM.

We aim to break down barriers and create a more inclusive STEAM community. Our initiatives are designed to empower women and girls with the skills and confidence they need to succeed in science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics.

Through our partnerships with schools, universities, and industry leaders, we strive to provide real-world experiences and connect our members with role models and mentors who can guide them on their journey.

"Education is the most powerful weapon which you can use to change the world." - Nelson Mandela

Why We Started

The founders of STEAMHers, Shreya Baronia and Maider Escobar, both experienced challenges and discrimination in STEAM fields due to their gender.

Shreya often felt alienated in male-dominated environments, while Maider recognized the necessity of creating opportunities for women in areas where there were few opportunities for them to drive meaningful change.

Together, they launched STEAMHers to combat these issues and to create a platform where women can thrive and support each other.

Our Vision

We envision a world where women are equally represented in all areas of STEAM.

Through our advocacy and initiatives, we aim to break down barriers that hinder women's progress in STEAM, whether they be cultural, systemic, or institutional. We envision a future where young girls grow up knowing that they have a place in STEAM careers and where established professionals find support and opportunities to advance their careers and leadership roles.

Ultimately, our vision extends beyond equality to equity, where resources, opportunities, and recognition are distributed fairly among all genders, ensuring that no woman is left behind in the march towards scientific and technological advancement.

Our Programs

We offer a variety of programs including online courses, workshops, competitions, and summer programs. These initiatives are designed to build confidence, enhance skills, and promote gender equality in STEAM fields.

Our vision is a world where women are equally represented in all areas of STEAM, contributing their unique perspectives and talents to drive innovation and progress.

STEAM Courses

Competition Math ▼

Advanced problem-solving techniques and competition preparation.

  • Focus on algebra, geometry, and number theory.
  • Strategies for solving complex problems quickly and efficiently.
  • Practice with past competition problems (e.g., AMC, MathCounts, and IMO).
  • Development of critical thinking and logical reasoning skills.
  • Individual and group problem-solving sessions to foster teamwork.
Geometry/Algebra 2/Trigonometry ▼

In-depth exploration of mathematical concepts and applications.

  • Geometry: Study of shapes, sizes, and properties of figures.
    • Euclidean and non-Euclidean geometry.
    • Properties of shapes, theorems, and proofs.
    • Coordinate geometry and transformations.
    • Geometric constructions and loci.
  • Algebra 2: Advanced algebraic concepts and techniques.
    • Polynomial, rational, exponential, and logarithmic functions.
    • Complex numbers and their properties.
    • Systems of equations and inequalities.
    • Matrices and determinants.
  • Trigonometry:
    • Trigonometric functions and their applications.
    • Graphs of sine, cosine, and tangent functions.
    • Trigonometric identities and equations.
    • Law of Sines and Law of Cosines.
    • Applications in real-world contexts, such as physics and engineering.
Neuroscience ▼

Understanding the brain's structure, function, and disorders.

  • Introduction to the nervous system and its components.
  • Study of brain anatomy and major brain regions.
  • Mechanisms of learning, memory, and behavior.
  • Exploration of neurological disorders and mental health conditions.
  • Discussion of cutting-edge neuroscience research and technologies.
  • Careers in neuroscience and related fields.
Web Development ▼

Building modern, responsive websites and web applications.

  • Introduction to HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.
  • Designing and developing user-friendly web interfaces.
  • Collaborative project development and teamwork skills.
  • Deploying web applications to the cloud.
  • Exploring career opportunities in web development.

STEAM Contests

We organize STEAM virtual competitions for girls and women, offering exciting prizes to the winners. These competitions are designed to inspire creativity, innovation, and a passion for STEAM subjects. Remember, "The future belongs to those who believe in the beauty of their dreams." Let's make those dreams a reality together!

Math Contest (Grades 6-8) ▼

A challenging math contest designed for middle schoolers.

  • 1. Contest Format: The contest consists of multiple-choice and open-ended questions. To enhance quick thinking, problem-solving sessions are timed.

  • 2. Types of Problems:

    • Algebra, geometry, number theory, and combinatorics.
    • Real-world applications of mathematical concepts.

  • 3. Skills Developed:

    • Critical thinking and analytical reasoning.
    • Collaboration and teamwork.
    • Time management and efficient problem-solving techniques.

  • 4. Preparatory Resources:

    • Access to sample problems and practice tests.
    • Workshops and tutoring sessions.

  • 5. Support and Prizes:

    • Mentorship from volunteer teachers.
    • Prizes for top performers.
    • Opportunities to advance to higher-level competitions.

Contest Date: August 4th

Physics Contest (Grades 9-11) ▼

  • 1. Contest Format: This contest consists of experimental and theoretical problem-solving.

  • 2. Topics Covered:

    • Mechanics, including kinematics, dynamics, and energy.
    • Electromagnetism and electrical circuits.
    • Thermodynamics and heat transfer.
    • Modern physics and quantum mechanics (basic concepts).

  • 3. Skills Developed:

    • Experimental design and data analysis.
    • Problem-solving under time constraints.
    • Critical thinking in physics applications.

  • 4. Benefits:

    • Recognition for achievement in physics.
    • Mentorship from volunteer teachers.
    • Prizes for top performers.

Summer Programs

Our summer programs offer intensive courses and exciting competitions, providing a perfect opportunity for girls to dive deep into STEAM subjects during their break.

Join us this summer and embrace the opportunity to learn, explore, and create in our intensive STEAM programs. Discover new passions and develop skills that will shape your future!

Duration: Typically held over three weeks, each class session spans 2-3 hours, allowing for in-depth exploration of STEAM topics.

Please note that while most of our courses are offered during the summer, some classes may not be available due to teacher availability.

We strive to offer a robust selection of courses that cater to diverse interests and skill levels, ensuring an enriching educational experience for all participants!

Shreya Baronia

Co-Founder

Through previous experiences in STEAM teams, I faced alienation and struggled to connect with the boys who would talk amongst themselves. To encourage more women to join and offset the large male influence in STEAM, I founded this organization with Maider.

Maider Escobar

Co-Founder

During my lifetime, I have learnt that women are pioneers, innovators and leaders. However, many women in STEAM remain unrecognised. In order to change this, I created STEAMHers with Shreya to empower women, celebrate their achievements and ensure their voices are heard.

Jina Mukherjee

Outreach Lead

As an Indian woman, discrimination and sexualization began in childhood. Despite leadership roles, I was talked over and laughed at by boys, dismissed for being a girl. I joined STEAMHers to help other girls realize they can, and will.

Angelisa Wang

Programming Lead

Growing up, I’ve always felt discriminated against in STEM because of my identity as a girl and I always thought it was unfair. By joining STEAMHers I hope to freely share my passion of STEM with others and help make stem more accessible to people all over the world.

Sabrina Tasnim Shoshee

Marketing Lead

I joined STEAMHers because I believe in the transformative power of education and equality. Growing up, I saw the immense potential in my peers that often went untapped due to a lack of resources and support. With this organization, I aim to help bridge the gender gap in STEAM fields.

Abigail Yankey

Competitions Lead

I like to play chess, solve puzzles, and research space. I joined STEAMHers as I would love to see more women in male-dominated STEM fields. Additionally, to motivate others to discover the creative aspects of STEM, I want to show STEM is engaging and accessible to everyone.
